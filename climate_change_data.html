Why does this matter?

From the graph, we can see that the baseline mean is 0 Celsius. Between 1880 and 1930, the mean temperature, per 10 years, is actual below the mean. At around the turn of the mid-1900s, the average mean temperature starts deviate and eventually, grows rapidly to the highest peak in 2016.

From between 800,000 years ago all the way to 200 years ago, the ppm (parts per million) of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere was always below 300ppm. About 100 years ago, this number went above the 300ppm threshold and has since been growing at an alarming rate.

The data speaks for itself; as human society began to modernize, our use of fossil fuels has lead to a huge spike in global carbon dioxide levels and global temperatures.
